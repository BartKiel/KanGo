Methodology — How This Map Was Built

4-step process: Rufus question harvesting → content auditing → gap mapping → t-SNE visualisation

1

Rufus Question Harvesting

We queried Amazon Rufus (Amazon's AI shopping assistant) on Amazon.co.uk with the seed query "spaghetti". Rufus returned 5 top-level (L1) questions — these are the main themes shoppers explore. Each L1 branched into L2 follow-ups, and each L2 into L3 deep-dives, yielding a hierarchical tree of 181 unique consumer questions across 3 levels.

5 L1 seed questions ~30 L2 follow-ups ~146 L3 deep-dives 181 total
2

Barilla UK Content Audit

We scraped and manually reviewed the entire barilla.com/en-gb website, cataloguing all available content assets across 6 content sources:

Spaghetti PDP
Product detail page — cook time, water ratio, heritage
Protein+ Campaign
20g protein/100g, 3 SKUs, consumer research, dedicated recipes
Passive Cooking
Sustainability campaign, timing guide PDF, Passive Cooker device
Recipe Hub
83 spaghetti recipes, filterable by product, no time labels
Red Sauces
8 sauce products — Italian tomatoes, no preservatives
Pesto Range
3 SKUs — Genovese, Rosso, Basilico e Rucola
3

Gap Classification & Scoring

Each of the 181 Rufus questions was manually assessed against Barilla UK content and classified into one of three statuses:

✅
Covered
Barilla UK website directly answers this question with dedicated content
🟡
Partial
Some relevant content exists but doesn't fully address the question
❌
Gap
No relevant content exists — Rufus cannot recommend Barilla for this query

Priority flagging: Questions marked 🔥 are high-impact gaps where (a) the question is a L1 or L2 level (higher visibility), (b) there is existing content that could be expanded, or (c) the topic represents high consumer demand with zero content.

Coverage Rate formula: (Covered + Partial × 0.5) ÷ Total × 100. Partial answers earn half credit. Current rate: 17%.

4

t-SNE Semantic Visualisation

All 181 questions were embedded as vectors using OpenAI text-embedding-3-small (1536 dimensions), then projected to 2D using t-SNE (perplexity=15). Questions that are semantically similar cluster together on the scatter map. This reveals that most red clusters (gaps) appear in cooking tips and sauce pairing zones — exactly where Barilla UK has the weakest content.

Why this matters for Rufus: Amazon Rufus uses semantic understanding to match customer queries to brand content. If Barilla's website doesn't contain content semantically relevant to these questions, Rufus will recommend competitors who do. Each green dot is a question where Barilla wins in Rufus; each red dot is a question going to competitors.

⚠️ Limitations & Notes

  • • Rufus questions were harvested on 29–30 Mar 2026. Rufus question trees can change over time as Amazon updates its AI.
  • • Content audit covers barilla.com/en-gb only — not Amazon product listing content (A+ pages, bullet points). A+ content could partially cover some gaps.
  • • Some L3 questions (konjac noodles, ancient grains) are outside Barilla's product scope and are labelled N/A — they still appear in the gap count but are not recommended for action.
  • • Priority flags are editorial judgement based on consumer demand signals and content feasibility, not quantitative traffic data.

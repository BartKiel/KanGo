📝 ARTICLE #2 — Commoditization
Image: art02_commoditisation.png | Link: webquestdigital.com/article-02-commoditisation.html
Ask ChatGPT to recommend a laundry detergent. It will probably say "Tide, Persil, or any major brand." No differentiation. No preference. Just a generic list. Now ask yourself: is your brand on that list? And even if it is — are you interchangeable with the others? This is the Commoditization Trap. When your messaging is generic, AI has no reason to distinguish you. The result? You become interchangeable. Another option in a flat list. The brands that win in the AI era are the ones that give AI a reason to recommend them specifically. 👇 We break down why this happens and what to do about it. 🔗 The Commoditization Trap: Why AI Treats Most Brands as Interchangeable https://webquestdigital.com/article-02-commoditisation.html #Branding #AIStrategy #Commoditization #WebQuestDigital

📝 ARTICLE #4 — GEO
Image: art04_geo.png | Link: webquestdigital.com/article-04-geo.html
You've heard of SEO. Meet GEO. SEO made your brand visible to Google. GEO — Generative Engine Optimization — makes your brand understandable to ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot, and every AI that answers consumer questions. The difference is fundamental: • SEO is about keywords → GEO is about meaning • SEO is about ranking → GEO is about being chosen • SEO is about links → GEO is about trusted context AI doesn't rank pages. It synthesizes knowledge. If your brand's digital footprint doesn't provide clear, structured, differentiated context — the AI will default to your competitor.
